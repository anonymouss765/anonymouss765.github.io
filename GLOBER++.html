GLOBER++: GLOBal Video Features as Guiding Beacon for CohERent Video Generation

Abstract: Video generation demands synthesizing videos with both global coherence and local realism. Current methods employ video auto-encoders to alleviate the computational burden, along with autoregression or interpolation strategies for generating multi-frame videos, yet they face challenges in maintaining information fidelity and video consistency. To address these issues, we propose GLOBER++, which employs a concurrent generation strategy and transforms the conventional video decoding process from deterministic to generative. The concurrent generation strategy decouples the generation of general video content and the synthesis of frame details, which enhances video coherence by utilizing a global video feature as a guiding beacon for multi-frame video generation and facilitates flexible synthesis of video frames by modeling continuous time and permitting concurrent synthesis of multiple video frames. Generative video decoding can recover the information loss during video encoding process, thus enabling a high compression rate. Moreover, we devise three mechanisms including frame-wise information streams, sampling space truncation, and recursive noise decomposition to enhance video consistency. Extensive experiments demonstrate the superiority of GLOBER++ over existing methods in terms of efficiency and effectiveness, achieving significant improvements across four challenging benchmarks.
