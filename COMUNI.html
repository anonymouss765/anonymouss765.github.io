COMUNI: Decomposing Common and Unique Video Signals for Diffusion-based Video Generation

Abstract: Since videos record objects moving coherently, adjacent video frames have commonness (similar object appearances) and uniqueness (slightly changed postures). Based on this insight, we propose a novel two-stage framework for diffusion-based video generation, i.e. COMUNI, which decomposes COMmon and UNIque video signals to eliminate redundancy. In the first stage, CU-VAE is proposed to decompose video signals and encode them to latent features. For self-supervisedly training, a cascading merge module is adopted to recompose video signals for video decoding. Then in the second stage, CU-LDM is proposed to model latent features for video generation. In particular, two specific diffusion streams are adopted to model common and unique latent features respectively, and additional joint modules are interpolated for joint modeling. For content consistency and motion coherence of generated videos, a novel embedding method is proposed to incorporate spatial and temporal absolute position information into joint modules. Extensive experiments demonstrate the superiority of our proposed method and the necessity of decomposing common and unique video signals for video generation.
